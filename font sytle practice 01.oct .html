The story of "fonts and words" refers to the relationship between the visual style of typography (fonts) and the content they convey (words). Fonts are chosen to complement a story's genre and maintain reader focus by minimizing distraction, with serif fonts like Garamond for classic novels and sans-serif fonts like Arial for modern designs. The font's historical roots trace back to the Old French word "fonte" for a casting or mould, highlighting its function in shaping the written word.
