ABC is the Alphabets in English

An alphabet is a writing system where standard symbols, called letters, represent the distinct sounds (phonemes) of a language, allowing for the formation of words and sentences. The English alphabet, consisting of 26 letters from A to Z, is a widely recognized example. Alphabets evolved from ancient Egyptian hieroglyphs and are a more efficient way to represent sounds compared to systems like syllabaries, which require symbols for each consonant-vowel combination.
